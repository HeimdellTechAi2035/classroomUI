# Safeguarding Policy

**Version:** 2.1  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Review Date:** January 2025

## 1. Purpose

RemoteAbility CIC is committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all adults who participate in our training programmes. This policy outlines our approach to safeguarding and the procedures we follow.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All RemoteAbility staff and trainers
- Volunteers
- Partner organisations
- Trainees and participants

## 3. Principles

Our safeguarding approach is based on the following principles:

1. **The welfare of trainees is paramount** - All decisions prioritise trainee wellbeing
2. **All adults have the right to be safe** - Regardless of background or circumstances
3. **All adults have the right to be treated with dignity and respect**
4. **Safeguarding is everyone's responsibility** - Not just designated leads
5. **Early intervention is key** - Concerns should be reported promptly

## 4. Recognising Abuse

Adults at risk may experience various forms of abuse:

- **Physical abuse** - Including assault, hitting, misuse of medication
- **Emotional/psychological abuse** - Threats, humiliation, isolation
- **Financial abuse** - Theft, fraud, exploitation
- **Neglect** - Failure to provide care or support
- **Discriminatory abuse** - Based on protected characteristics
- **Online abuse** - Cyberbullying, harassment, exploitation

### Signs to Watch For
- Unexplained changes in behaviour
- Withdrawal or fear around certain people
- Unexplained injuries
- Financial irregularities
- Signs of self-neglect
- Disclosure of abuse

## 5. Reporting Concerns

### What to Do

If you have a safeguarding concern:

1. **Listen** - Take the person seriously
2. **Don't investigate** - This is not your role
3. **Record** - Write down what you observed/were told
4. **Report** - Contact the Designated Safeguarding Lead immediately

### In an Emergency

## 6. Confidentiality

- Information will be shared on a need-to-know basis
- Trainees will be informed when concerns are raised
- We cannot promise complete confidentiality if safety is at risk
- Records are kept securely and in line with data protection law

## 7. Staff and Trainer Responsibilities

All trainers must:
- Attend safeguarding training (refreshed annually)
- Know who the Designated Safeguarding Lead is
- Report concerns promptly
- Maintain appropriate boundaries
- Follow the code of conduct

## 8. Online Safety

When delivering remote training:
- Use approved platforms only
- Maintain professional boundaries online
- Report concerning online behaviour
- Support trainees with digital safety

## 9. Record Keeping

All safeguarding concerns and actions must be recorded:
- Use the secure safeguarding log in the classroom system
- Include: date, time, concern, action taken
- Records are encrypted and access-controlled

## 10. Review

This policy is reviewed annually or when:
- Legislation changes
- Following a serious incident
- Best practice guidance is updated
