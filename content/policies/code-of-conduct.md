# Code of Conduct

**Version:** 1.2  
**Last Updated:** January 2024

## Our Commitment

RemoteAbility CIC is committed to providing a safe, welcoming, and supportive learning environment for all participants. This Code of Conduct outlines the standards of behaviour expected from everyone involved in our training programmes.

## Expected Behaviour

### Respect
- Treat all participants, trainers, and staff with respect
- Value diversity and different perspectives
- Use appropriate and professional language
- Listen actively when others are speaking

### Professionalism
- Attend sessions on time and prepared
- Notify trainers in advance if you cannot attend
- Complete assignments and activities to the best of your ability
- Dress appropriately for video calls

### Online Conduct
- Keep your camera on when possible (we understand if you need a break)
- Mute your microphone when not speaking
- Use appropriate backgrounds (or blur if needed)
- Don't share meeting links without permission
- Be patient with technical difficulties

### Communication
- Be constructive in feedback and discussions
- Ask questions - there are no silly questions!
- Respect confidentiality of group discussions
- Use official communication channels

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following will not be tolerated:
- Harassment of any kind
- Discriminatory language or behaviour
- Bullying or intimidation
- Disruptive behaviour during sessions
- Sharing inappropriate content
- Breach of confidentiality

## If Things Go Wrong

### If You Experience Issues
- Speak to your trainer
- Contact the programme team
- Use the anonymous feedback option

### Consequences
Breaches of this code may result in:
- Informal discussion and reminder
- Formal warning
- Temporary suspension from sessions
- Removal from the programme (in serious cases)

## Support

We're here to help you succeed. If you're struggling with any aspect of the programme, please reach out. We can:
- Adjust pace or approach
- Provide additional support
- Connect you with external services if needed

## Questions?

If anything in this code is unclear, please ask your trainer for clarification.
