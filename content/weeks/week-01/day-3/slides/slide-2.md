# Main Learning Activity - Understanding Confidence
*Duration: 40 minutes*

## Activity Overview
**Rebuilding Confidence Through Understanding**

This is our main learning block where we explore what confidence really is and how it can be rebuilt.

---

## Trainer Script

"Let's talk about confidence. I want to start with what confidence is NOT:

**Confidence is NOT:**
- Something you're born with or without
- Being loud or assertive
- Never feeling nervous
- Always knowing what to say
- Being the same in every situation

**Confidence IS:**
- Feeling safe to be yourself
- Knowing you have a right to take up space
- Trusting that your thoughts and feelings matter
- Being willing to try, even when it's uncomfortable
- Growing through practice and experience"

---

## Key Teaching Points

### 1. How Confidence Gets Affected (10 minutes)

**Past Work Experiences:**
- Being dismissed or ignored
- Having ideas shot down
- Experiencing workplace bullying
- Feeling incompetent or 'stupid'
- Being made to feel invisible

**Health and Setbacks:**
- Mental health challenges
- Physical health issues
- Long periods away from work
- Life changes and transitions
- Financial stress and worry

**The Impact:**
"These experiences don't make you weak. They're normal human responses to difficult situations. The important thing is that confidence can be rebuilt."

### 2. How Confidence Grows (15 minutes)

**Through Safety:**
- Environments where you won't be judged
- People who listen without interrupting
- Spaces where mistakes are okay
- Knowing you can ask for help

**Through Repetition:**
- Practicing in low-stakes situations
- Building up gradually
- Celebrating small wins
- Learning from gentle feedback

**Through Small Wins:**
- Achieving manageable goals
- Being acknowledged for efforts
- Having positive experiences
- Building evidence that you can succeed

### 3. Communication Styles - There's No Single 'Right' Way (15 minutes)

**Verbal Communication:**
- Some people think out loud
- Prefer face-to-face conversation
- Like immediate interaction
- Process ideas through talking

**Written Communication:**
- Some people need time to think
- Prefer email or chat
- Like to edit their thoughts
- Feel more confident in writing

**Structured Communication:**
- Some people need agendas
- Prefer planned discussions
- Like to prepare in advance
- Work better with clear frameworks

**Paced Communication:**
- Some people need processing time
- Prefer slower conversations
- Like pauses between topics
- Need time to formulate responses

"None of these is better than the others. The key is finding what works for YOU and communicating in environments that support your style."

---

## Interactive Element

"Take a moment to think: Which of these communication styles feels most comfortable to you? You might be a mix of several, and that's perfectly normal.

Type in the chat or tell us: What communication style feels most 'you'?"

*Allow 3-4 minutes for responses and brief discussion*

---

## Compliance Notes
- Normalize all communication preferences
- Watch for self-criticism or negative self-talk
- Validate experiences of workplace difficulties
- Emphasize that there are no wrong answers
